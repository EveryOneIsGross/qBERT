You can only trust your enemy once.

That's a classic piece of cautionary wisdom. It implies:

Inherent Risk: Trusting an enemy goes against the basic nature of the relationship. An enemy, by definition, has interests opposed to yours.

One Chance for Betrayal: If you extend trust to an enemy and they betray it, you've learned a definitive (and likely painful) lesson. There's no reason to give them a second chance to do the same.

One Chance for (Temporary) Alliance: Perhaps the "once" refers to a unique situation where necessity forces a temporary alignment or reliance. Even if they don't betray you that one time, the fundamental opposition remains, making future trust deeply unwise. The circumstances that allowed for that single instance of trust are unlikely to repeat, or relying on it again would be foolish.

Essentially, it's a stark warning: extending trust to someone fundamentally opposed to you is exceptionally dangerous, and if you do it, you likely won't get (or shouldn't risk) a second opportunity. Any trust placed is fragile and likely situational, not a foundation for an ongoing relationship.
